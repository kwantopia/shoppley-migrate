{% trans "About Shoppley" %}

{% blocktrans %} Shoppley's goal is to bring you personalized just-in-time offers you will want from your local area. {% endblocktrans %}

Shoppley is currently in alpha pilot and we would like your feedback. Shoppley is created by a team of MIT and RISD alumni to help small businesses and customers create a more sustainable relationship and utilize their time and resources more efficiently.
Team Members
Kwan Hong Lee
Kwan loves reinventing the role of mobile phones in our lives. Kwan has industry experience in personal finance (Bank of America, Intuit), consumer electronics (Bose) and telecommunications (GTE, Samsung). Most recently his Ph.D. dissertation is on social transactions that investigates impacts of mobile communication and social media on purchase decisions. At the MIT Media Lab he ignited the Center for Future Banking by initiating a relationship with Bank of America. He has experience working with various industry partners to run real world experiments with human participants and have led 4 research projects that led to release of 4 mobile applications in the App Store and Android Market for his research projects. He has B.S., M.Eng. degree in computer science from Cornell University, M.S. degree from the MIT Media Lab and finishing his Ph.D. dissertation in Viral Spaces group at MIT Media Lab.
Dawei Shen
Dawei loves large sized data sets. Dawei obtained his bachelor degree in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University in 2003, then earned separate masters’ degrees in High Performance Computing and Electrical Engineering from National University of Singapore and University of Notre Dame. He is currently a researcher and a Ph.D. candidate in the Viral Spaces group at the MIT Media Lab, with research also in the MIT Communications Future Program (CFP) and MIT Center for Future Banking (CFB). He has 4 U.S. patents pending and 10+ peer-reviewed publications. In addition to his academic experiences, Dawei has spent time working at Institute for Infocomm Research Singapore, Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, IBM Research, and Facebook. He has extensive experiences in product design, innovative system development, and large software management. His Ph.D. project “Barter information markets” won the runner-up position in the prestigious MIT $100K competition, and a finalist in the $100K Linked-Data competition.
Douglas Hwang
Douglas loves food and business. Douglas Hwang, is a recent MBA graduate at MIT Sloan, where he focused on innovation, entrepreneurship, and applying analytics to business challenges. This past summer, Douglas was at Apple, working project management and supply chain operations for new products. Prior to MIT Sloan, Douglas was a jet engine engineer for GE Aviation and a graduate of the Edison Engineering Leadership Development Program. Outside of school, he can be found playing basketball, skiing, and getting lost in the great outdoors. Douglas holds a Bachelors of Science in Mechanical Engineering from MIT, and a Masters of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Tufts University.
Boram Yi
Boram is a socialite and can make friends with anybody. Boram is currently working full time as a user experience / visual designer for internal web applications at a pharmaceutical company to provide more streamlined methods for drug discovery pipeline. She graduated from RISD with a BFA degree in Industrial Design in 2010. While at RISD, Boram constantly worked on entrepreneurial product design and development projects, collaborating with other students from Sloan School of Business at MIT, Brown University, Babson College and Olin College of Engineering. The business plan for a high-tech toy company, academically mentored by Hasbro Inc and developed with Brown University students, was selected as one of finalists in the RI Business Plan Competition in 2009. She participated in the RI Center for Innovation & Entrepreneurship Summer Incubator Program in 2009. Samples of her work can be found at www.boramyi.com.
Yod Watanaprakornkul
Yod loves speed. Yod is currently an M.Eng. candidate in computer science at MIT. He is interested in high-performance computer systems and mobile applications. Yod has released four iPhone applications in the Apple App Store working as an undergraduate researcher in the Viral Spaces Group at MIT Media Lab. In summer 2010, Yod worked at Facebook to develop profiling tools for front-end web performance. He has a B.S. in computer science from MIT.
Seak Meng Lay
Meng loves computationally difficult problems. Seak Meng Lay has a B.S. in computer science and a minor in Mathematics from MIT. He is interested in artificial intelligence and machine learning. In summer 2010, he interned at the Robert Bosch Research Center. He used learning algorithms to build an adaptive navigation system. Last winter, Seak Meng’s team built an autonomous pokerbot to compete with other teams and won the MIT pokerbot competition. He also did his senior thesis with Dawei on “Barter information markets.”
Eunice Giarta
Eunice is truly interdisciplinary. Eunice is currently working towards her S.B. in Computer Science and Engineering at MIT alongside a minor in Management Science with Finance. Her interest in social media and previous work through a web development UROP under Kwan Hong Lee with the Viral Communications Group at the MIT Media Lab (Summer 2010) propelled her entrepreneurial spirit. Eunice interned at One William Street Capital Management in NYC (IAP 2011) and is currently a Quantitative Management Intern at Bank of America with the Global Portfolio Strategies group. She is fascinated by the fluidity and symbiotic-nature of the relationship between the fields of mathematics and computer science with business and finance. She hopes to incorporate her technical experience and eye for design with her interest in the business and financial markets.
